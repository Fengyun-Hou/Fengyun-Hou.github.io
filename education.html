PhD in Psychology

University of Bristol

2021 - Present

Supervisors: Professor Nina Kazanina (University of Geneva), Professor Markus Damian (University of Bristol), Dr Alexander Anderson (University of Bristol)

Research Focus: Neural mechanisms underlying language processing and memory representations of language using frequency-tagging EEG

In this journey:

My doctoral research journey began with exploring the fundamental question of how relational information in language contributes to memory organization. This path has led me through advanced EEG methodologies, cross-linguistic studies, and collaborative research with leading institutions. The journey continues to evolve as I investigate the neural mechanisms that underlie our ability to process and remember linguistic structures, bridging cognitive psychology, neuroscience, and psycholinguistics.

2016 - 2019

Supervisors: Professor Xin Jiang (Beijing Language and Culture University), Professor Qingqing Qu (Chinese Academy of Sciences)

Thesis: "Interference effects of radical markings and stroke order animations on Chinese character learning among L2 learners: Evidence from reaction time and ERP studies"

In this journey:

My master's journey marked my first deep dive into experimental psychology and neurolinguistics. This period was transformative as I learned to combine behavioral measurements with electrophysiological techniques. The experience of investigating how visual elements affect language learning opened my eyes to the intricate relationship between perception, cognition, and language processing, setting the foundation for my current doctoral research.
