Welcome!

I am a PhD student specializing in Cognitive Neuroscience of Language. My research investigates how the brain processes and represents relational structures in both linguistic and visual input, with particular focus on how language input is encoded and stored in memory.

Currently pursuing my doctorate at the University of Bristol's School of Psychological Science, I use advanced electrophysiological methods, particularly frequency-tagging EEG, to understand the neural mechanisms underlying relational processing in language and vision.

My work bridges cognitive psychology, neuroscience, and psycholinguistics, exploring fundamental questions about how relational information is processed across different modalities and how it contributes to memory organization and retrieval.
