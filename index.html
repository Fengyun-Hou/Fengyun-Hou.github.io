Welcome to my academic homepage

I am a researcher specializing in psycholinguistics and cognitive neuroscience of language. My work explores how the human brain processes, understands, and produces language, bridging the gap between cognitive psychology and neuroscience.

Currently based at the University of Bristol's School of Psychological Science, I investigate the neural mechanisms underlying language comprehension, the cognitive processes involved in bilingual language processing, and the relationship between language and thought.

My research employs various methodologies including behavioral experiments, neuroimaging (fMRI, EEG), and computational modeling to understand the complexities of human language processing.
